A Canvas of Culture

Explore the rich tapestry of Indian folk art. Each style tells a unique story of its region, culture, and people.

Our Curated Artforms

Warli Painting

Originating from the Warli tribe of Maharashtra, this art form uses geometric shapes like circles, triangles, and squares to depict scenes of daily life, rituals, and nature. The monochromatic style creates a powerful and minimalist aesthetic.

Learn More

Madhubani Painting

Also known as Mithila art, this style is characterized by its intricate line drawings filled with bright, bold colors. Traditionally created by women in the Mithila region of Bihar, the paintings often depict mythological tales and Hindu deities.

Learn More

Pithora Painting

Practiced by the Rathwa and Bhilala tribes of Gujarat and Madhya Pradesh, Pithora paintings are ritualistic artworks created on the walls of homes. They are distinguished by their vibrant colors and depiction of joyful events and revered figures, especially horses.

Learn More

Tanjore Painting

A classical South Indian art form from Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu. These panel paintings are known for their rich colors, glittering gold foil overlays, and intricate inlay work with semi-precious stones, typically depicting divine figures.
